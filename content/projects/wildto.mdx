## Overview

Wild TO is an interactive dashboard that turns Toronto's open **311 service request data** into a story about how people and urban wildlife share the city. Raccoons, coyotes, foxes, skunks — every call to 311 becomes a data point, mapped and analyzed to reveal where, when, and why human–wildlife encounters happen.

## What It Shows

By aggregating and visualizing thousands of 311 requests, the dashboard surfaces patterns that are invisible at the level of a single complaint — seasonal spikes, neighbourhood hotspots, and the geography of cohabitation across Toronto.

## Approach

- **Open data pipeline** — ingest and clean Toronto's public 311 service request datasets
- **Geospatial analysis** — aggregate requests by location to map wildlife activity across the city
- **Interactive visualization** — explorable charts and maps that let users filter by species, time, and area
- **Editorial framing** — a narrative layer that frames the data as a question of cohabitation, not just nuisance

## Stack

Built with **Next.js** (App Router) and a D3-driven visualization layer, styled with Playfair Display and JetBrains Mono for an editorial, data-forward feel.
